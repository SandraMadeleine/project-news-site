What's up in the Nordics?

Are you in the Nordic region and want to know what fun stuff is happening near you?

The Nordic countries are full of surprises and strange customs, and there is always something fun going on, regardless of which country you have decided to visit. Check out our articles on the latest happenings in the Nordic country of your choice. Our Scandi-correspondents have made sure to find the most fun and quirky news from all over the Scandinavian countries, to ensure that there is something for everyone. Want to see how the Danish celebrate the first week of March? You'll find it here. Interested in what fun stuff you can eat in Norway when you visit? Yes, you'll find that here. Can you also find any fun outdoor activity in Sweden which includes animals? Of course, just click around and you'll hit the jackpot!

Welcoming spring with happy Danish people

Did you know that the Danish have a tradition of celebrating the onset of spring by a series of funny customs, all taking place in the first week of March? Neither did we. If you happen to be in Denmark during this week, you can expect to see many vendors selling a very strong minty drink, to be had together with a bun of really spicy pölse, the two are to be enjoyed together with family and friends in the sunshine that we haven't seen for so long. If you are fortunate, you might also come across the tradition of pop-up auctions in a garden near you. Intrigued? Click this article to find out more about what our teak-obsessed neighbours are up to in a few weeks time, all in the spirit of "hygge".

Come join the reindeer-run in Stockholm!

A reindeer? In Stockholm? Isn't that a bit too far south? Yes, but reindeer can travel too you know, ever heard of Rudolph?. If you have no idea what to do this weekend and you want to experience something new, you can head out to Gärdet to watch a reindeer-run. Not with a sled behind the reindeer but with humans on mini-skis. Sound wild? It is! The contestants will need to complete a course with twists and turns, while skiing behind their reindeer. Fastest to complete the course wins. And don't worry, this is not F1, to avoid injuries the contestants race the course one at a time. This event is sure to make you both laugh, cheer, and go "aaawww". And yes, you may pet the reindeer after the race.

Can you actually eat that? Yes, if you are in Norway you can!

Let's be honest here, Norway has not really been highlighted for its cuisine, with the national dish said to be the supermarket freeze-box pizza Grandiosa. That aside, there is a dish not many of us have heard of. If you are in Norway in the winter, check out our guide on where to find the secret, but delicious, lefse-burger. The burger is made with an algae that only grows in the winter months, and has to be harvested by breaking the ice open in order to pick it up. Though the combination of lefse and algae, in a burger, might sound less than exciting at first glance, it is actually quite the taste sensation...

Ice-festival coming up in Reykjavik

Much like the ice-hotel in northern Sweden, you can check-in to sleep on your very own icy bed in Reykjavik during the yearly ice-festival. Aside from an ice hotel, of course equipped with an ice bar as well, there will be ice carving contests and much more. For those with a need for speed, there will be a chance to ride in a real bobsleigh at high speeds, together with professionals. The bobsleigh course is beautifully located just outside the city centre, with very scenic views around the course. For the little ones the will be ice rinks for skating, with the Icelandic olympic gold champions of ice hockey visting, as well as a figure skating performance by the national figure skating champions to the hit song Husavik from the movie Eurovision Song Contest - the story of Fire saga.

Go kitesurfing on the Faroe Islands

While the Faroe Islands might not be on the top ten most visited places for holidaying in the Scandics, it is actually a really good spot for those who enjoy kitesurfing. It is ideally located as there is hardly a day without wind, and waves are plenty and fun. No, it isn't Mallorca, but if you make sure to dress right you will have a blast. Just make sure you are not having too much fun with the winds, or they might carry you back to Denmark! As the daredevils we are, we of course went there for a crash course on how to kitesurf, in an enviromemnt hard to find elsewhere. There is even a kitesurfing history museum downtown, for those who want to dive even deeper into the sport.

Kokkola brings the good stuff

If you find yourself in the vicinity of Kokkola in February, you should make a stop and explore this little gem. Every February, Kokkola hosts a food festival where you can sample different Finnish-Swedish crossover food, as well as well known delicacies from both countries. Word is out that the Karelian pirogues with a Swedish meatball filling are not to be missed. They are served with brown sauce dip and make an excellent street food dish. For those of you who have yet to try Memma, this is the place to be. Though traditionally served around Easter, this is the early-bird version and it is served both with the more traditional cream, as well as the more contemporary version as part of a baked Alaska.

Swim race on Svalbard

For those who dare, the annual swim race taking place in the harbour of Longyearbyen is coming up. The event draws a huge crowd and international participants, who all eagerly get in the less than warm water to have a 8 km swim around the harbor waters. It is not only the cold water that poses an obstacle to the contestants, but also the fact that there might be a surprise polar bear when you least expect it. On the more positive side, the fauna here also ups your chances of seeing a beluga whale and perhaps even a walrus. Puffin birds are plenty and you will see their bright orange feet whether you are in the water or safely watching from land.

Drive-in movies in Mariehamn

Every Saturday evening, from March to October, you can take your car and head for the drive-in movie set up in a huge parking lot in Mariehamn. Movie buffs without a car are more than welcome as well, and there is space to set up a blanket or a camping chair. They even have a popcorn wagon which will go from car to car before the movie starts. Need a refill mid-movie? No worries, just order in the app and the vendor will come to your car. Sounds sweet, right? We have the full program listed and it is a mix of new and old, to suit all tastes. Make sure to check it out!
